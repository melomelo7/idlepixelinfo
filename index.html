    Q1 : I cant gain Exp in Mining / Crafting ... how to get more ?
    A1 : You click on ore (Mining) / bars (Crafting), to see the option for
    converting item into Exp using Stardust
    This option may be locked if you did not progress far enough to unlock skills
    or do quests but it will pop soon enough ...
    Q2 : mining skill ... why bother leveling up ?
    Things you craft like machinery that you use in mining tab
    will have a level requirement to start using it.
    also the chisel tool will randomly help you found Geodes IF
    you have met the mining level(s) required.
    Q3 : Is Fishing or Finding seeds automatic?! Am I doing something wrong?
    A3 : (If you did unlock the skills)
    You fish and find seeds automatically. It's quite slow early game. For fishing, as you gain levels
    and get more fishing tools this speeds up quite a lot. For finding seeds, its always quite slow but gathering
    in the fields and upgrading your rake improves the seed rates substantially.
    Q4 : Energy is so hard to get! How do I solve my energy problem???
    A4 : You don't. Energy is a persistent problem through the entire game. That being said, in gathering you unlock
    Fruit Tree fertilizer. This is a substantial upgrade to your energy income and is critical in getting. Additionally
    upgrading your oven, socketing your fishing tools (so they get more exp and in turn catch more fish) and running
    the ‚ÄúFields‚Äù combat zone are all great ways to help mitigate your energy problems. Fields has 3 mobs who drop energy
    when fought and is a very cheap way to combat when energy is scarce.
    Q5 : Any best order to upgrade tools ?
    A5 : tough question kinda player taste related ...
    now we may consider not too bad of a choice :
    - [Brewing Kit] and [Axe]
    - [Rake] and [Hammer] ...
    - others in whatever order maybe ?

    Progression

    This section is just a Kick start as many people ask how to unlock basic stuff
    like SKILLS ... More infos to be found in QUESTS section. Then game Chat !!

    1. Buy Pickaxe from game shop
      (almost all is good to buy in game shop, when you have the coins go for it)

    3. craft all/anything you able to, from craft list
      (furnace will be the mother of your bars, eating your ores in the process..)
    4. Do Gem Seeker Quest
    5. sell some mining and/or crafting stuff to buy more in shop
    6. Buy Leather then craft Gloves [unlocks Gathering skill]
    7. Do Achievements Quest [unlocking Farming skill] in the process
    8. from now on seeds may be found at random pace and available inside farming tab
    9. during crafting process turn on/off machines when needs be : oil refill
    10. do eggplant quest (stardust expert) to[unlock Brewing skill]
    11. [unlock cooking skill] (by crafting the bronze oven)
    12. buy a weapon for next quest and to open combat MELEE
      (sell 500 stone and sell 250 coppers will match a weapon price btw)
    13. dont forget to click(equip) on the sword ... and try the fight
    14. this has triggered the next quest
    15. do the ent quest : kill it to [unlock Woodcutting skill]
    16. do Faradox's Introduction quest will [unlock Healing spell]
    17. fight in the fields to get enough string and craft a fishing net that will
      help launching the next quest : the fisherman ... doing it will [unlock Fishing skill]
    18. crafting the 1st community center will load the game shop with 3 new items:
      • arcade ticket 1000 gold (spam click rock for some money)

    Basics :
    Idle Pixel runs on a tick system.
    You'll learn the rhythm of it the more you play.

    There are 2 major portions to combat :
    • Your gear and stats
    • Your spells
    The game starts very basically then grows more complex.
    Until the ‚ÄúVolcano‚Äù combat area, the combat is very simple.
    No micro-management or strategy used outside of ‚Äúclick & heal‚Äù.
    (!)Kindly not pay too much attention to sets of bow-arrows-shield at once
    Additionally in this game there are 3 types of mobs :
    • Common Mobs
    • [R]are mobs (~20% chance)
    • Shiny mobs (not available until a specific quest, chance to meet 1%)

    Once EASY combat achievement is completed, every 10lv of combined melee/range
    will add some permanent 1HP Max upgrade, and magic will do the same to mana.

    The effective (but boring) "magic level training" is this :
    sit in the FIELDS with a bow and **no-arrow** to be able to use the highest spells
    availables as many times as possible before fight ends.
    (Game wont let you enter a fight with no-arrow but you may build a preset without
    OR exit fight to take off arrows)

    Starting from Volcano, the dumb "hit-and-heal" combat process will switch to
    using combat presets and try to fit the best action with best timing ...

    Presets are Button binded equippement selections you made and saved.
    Up to 5 presets available.
    During a fight pressing a button will thus swap to desired preset

      2. Ice Golem :

        (a no shield preset guarantee a hit thus guarantee a reflected damage)

      3. Ice Witch :
        Cast Fire twice, Reflect when she shakes at 1Hp
        Cast fire twice more ... and pray that all ends well ...

        So the fight starts, you DON'T cast any spells. When he shakes, reflect the very 1st freeze.
        Still don't cast any spells. From now on when he is able to freeze you, just break out with fire
        Having the long bow and possibly accuracy ring(+2?) equipped will also make the fight easier.

    Haunted Mansion :
    • - You come to Mansion after obviously getting everything from NF
      - Coming here you should be around 32hp and at 19 mana
      - There's no gimmick here unlike in past games where you needed ghost scan
      - I wouldn't start doing Mansion without all T1 and T2 rings, I'd even wait
      for full T3 Damage and Defense
      - Additionally you need to have Spiked Club, Longbow isn't necessary at all
      - The last thing you need before you start is a Ghost Jar
      this is to unlock the invisibility spell which is required for the Reaper
      and makes the Exorcist far easier
      - the Mansion adds an Item Store after your first win
      currency used is the Ghost Essence (GE) which can be earned
      at the rate of : 1 fight => random 100 ~ 300 GE
      - Strongly recommended to buy the Scythe as soon as possible

      - this area will bring another Spell : Invisibility
      by beating a ghost AND owning a ghost jar you will trap the ghost inside
      the spell is then unlocked/crafted using 666 GE
      ** This spell will give you roughly 2 seconds of being completely untargetable.
      The spell has a 30 second cooldown and costs 2 mana. This opens up a less grueling
      magic EXP grind where you can cast both Invisibility and Reflect for faster combats!
      (NOTE : its still more exp/fight to use only reflect but using invisibility makes the
      fights 3x faster and less insanity causing)

      - also a spell upgrade for Healing spell here ... same exp earned or mana invested
      but healing effect is tripled !

    • Items in store :
      1. Mana Crystal : 500 GE
      2. Health Crystal : 1 000 GE
      3. Haunted Bow : 5 000 GE
      4. Scythe : 10 000 GE
      5. Piece of Robe Set : 2 000 GE

    • Use Fight Presets : Each being back to full bat armor, except last : naked !
      1. Melee : Spiked Club (Later replaced with Scythe) + Shield
      2. Magic : Staff + Shield (armor replaced later with Robe)
      3. Ranged : Bow (short until swap long one) + Shield + Fire Arrows
      4. Melee-Naked : Spiked Club and nothing else (to use while reflecting against Ghost and Grandma)

    • Monster strategy :
      1. Ghost :
        His fight is relatively simple. Its a tank and spank,
        he occasionally goes invisible and thus unhittable through out the fight.
        Use fire and reflect on cooldown while he's visible.
        Naked reflect for more consistent damage. Pray.

      2. Grandma :
        Grandma is a deceptively hard fight. If you look at her stats, she seems
        to have low damage, low hp and low defense. The caveat is that every 3/4 ticks
        (I'm not entirely sure), she heals for 3 hp. So it's also a DPS race. You kinda
        just hope to hit big club rolls, spam fire and naked reflect. You can also heal
        if you feel you have the spare mana.

      3. Exorcist :
        Exorcist doesn't have standard auto attacks. Instead she attacks you with 100% accuracy,
        50+ damage attacks. This fight is a severe DPS race. Reflect her first attack, dodge the
        second using invisibility and spam fire on cooldown. If shes not dead by time the third
        attack comes, you lose. I believe you can make 3 club swings in this fight.
        If you want, you can opt into using the bow for faster attacks to be "safe".

      4. Reaper :
        The Reaper is a rough but simple fight. When his eyes glow red, you have to mitigate
        the next attack. Reflect > Invisibility for this. The reflect bounces back the damage
        and is more valuable, you'll have to use both. When using the invisibility to dodge,
        you must count to 2 before clicking it. Otherwise you'll invisibility too early and not dodge.
        During the whole fight you must spam improved heal on cooldown if you aren't at max.

      Main Drops : ** this area mobs reward GE with potential Guardian Key **
      Ghost : Invisibility Spell

      Grandma : Heal Upgrade

      Exorcist : Oxygen Mask / Oxygen Tank

      Reaper[R] : Scythe / Robe parts
